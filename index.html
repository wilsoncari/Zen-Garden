Style Guide 1.0

Purpose/Theme

The theme we have chosen is "Modern Plum". We want to incorporate shades of plum colors, bold serif and sans-serif fonts, and clean lines. Although our color pallette is somewhat neutral, the various shades of plum add a pop of color that is both modern and chic. The combination in typography of serif and sans-serif fonts pair well together and create interest on the page. Both fonts are easily readable and eye catching. The bold serif font we chose will help lead the viewers through the hierarchy of the page. As you can see from our wireframes, we are using a simple design with clean lines and paragraphs that will make users navigation a more enjoyable experience.

HTML Elements

Header:
The Header will have a background of $color-2. It will hold our logo or "CSS Zen Garden" name and a few small images to resemble the wireframes.
The background color of the header will $color-1.
We will try the logo in the Header first. If the logo doesn't look good on the header, we will write "Zen Garden" using $secondary-font in $color-4. We will then write "the beauty of css design" using $primary-font and $color-3 and lower case $primary-font (like the wireframes). We will then place 3 modern images for the large screen and 1 for the small.
Main div:
The background color of the entire page will be $color-2.
Paragraph or Info Boxes:
The background color for info boxes will be a combination of $color-1 and $color-2. Mainly $color-2, but if there are two side by side, the left one will be $color-2 and the right one will be $color-2.
All info boxes will use $primary-font for paragraphs and $secondary-font for headings. Headings should use $color-1 and paragraph text should use $color-2.
Navigation:
The background color of the nav box will be $color-1.
Navigation lists will have no bullet points and we will use $primary-font and $color-4 for the links.
The hover link color will be $color-3.
The active link will be white and underlined (1px).
The visited link will be $color-2.
Figures:
Images should be properly aligned (according to wireframe specifications).
Depending on the images, adding a border or box shadow may be appropriate.
